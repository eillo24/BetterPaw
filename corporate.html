Persona

Brand Name

The brand name “BetterPaw” consists of two words; ‘Better’ and ‘Paw’. ‘Better’, is the word used for wanting your dog to get better, be better, and feel better. It’s the word used where the owner wants to grow together with their pet friend and connect with them and grow a stronger bond with. ‘Better’ is where you overcome the struggles that you and your dog have, in order to live, love, and strive for another day, one paw at a time. ‘Better’ is where not only a dog learns how to better themselves, but where the caregiver also gets taught how to regain control. Whereas ‘Paw’ stands not only for the dog themself, but also lending a helping and loving hand. ‘Paw’ represents the joint efforts of both the owner, the dog, and the hardships they may have carried and continue to battle together. The word ‘Paw’ is about learning together, growing together, overcoming together. It’s a shared experience, where one cannot be without the other. ‘Paw’ is where love is a bond that is so strong that it can overcome anything through time and patience. With this, we created “BetterPaw”, where we provide online training for rescue dogs and their beloved owners.

Visual identity

BetterPaw’s logo incorporates both the dog, and a human ‘paw’, the hand. This brings it back to the explanation of the brand name, in which we want to return to our values, most prominently love and patience. They’re crucial visions for both of us, dog owners, and their rescue dogs. When looking at our logo, we not only want it to be recognisable, but also a logo that people look at and immediately understand what we stand for. The logo of BetterPaw is the base of every visual communication and will be put in every post that is put out, and it is important that we keep the colors of the logo the same as the colors of our palette, in addition to the font of the name, and if necessary, our slogan which is done with the Bilo font.

Our use of icons is on the softer side. We use round corners, simplistic design and visuals, and keep it calm this way. Paws and bones are used in a playful way (a trail of paws, with the bones used as a complimentary design to accompany it), whereas our buttons are kept simple with purely rounded edges. The social media icons are all rounded and kept quite simple, in order not to overwhelm people. Most of our icon usage are the paws. We use these as dividers and complimentary design with the copy and imagery.

As for our imagery, we want to capture the values of our brand. Such images have to demonstrate the importance, such as ‘Love’ showcasing the nurturing aspect of being a dog owner and the strong bond they have with their rescue dog. For ‘Patience’, the imagery should reflect the time and effort spent raising and training a dog and showing a supportive and most importantly a controlled environment. ‘Growth’ is an important imagery to capture the real-life situation that happens when being a dog owner, but being able to overcome obstacles together. And lastly, the imagery for ‘Community’ is most important of all, this should showcase the importance of being in a community and feeling understood. For this, BetterPaw uses a lifestyle photography in authentic settings, either at home or outside. The imagery should display the importance of our vision, values, and capture the spirit and energy of BetterPaw. We show real product interaction, positive environments, real life situations that reflect BetterPaw honestly, portray people in thriving and enjoyable situations, and people in bright and natural lighting. We do not show staged interactions or exaggerate effects/poses, nor do we use out-of-focus imagery. Overly conceptual imagery can be used, but in caution depending on what message BetterPaw wants to send.

BetterPaw also makes great use of artistic visuals, as they are popular in terms of usage in our target group. These visuals showcase both dogs and dog owners, with a focus on the dogs. They are of simplistic design and do not take away from the importance at hand, our message and copy that comes with it. As BetterPaw uses a colorful palette alongside its funky font and core values, it is important that the brand showcases this through visuals as well. Digitally drawn and simple visuals are considered the best option for this. The feeling these visuals evoke are positive and should only be used when sending out a positive message. The visuals themselves should showcase this as well through smiles, playful behaviour, and overall happiness.

Copywriting

The tone of voice for BetterPaw is friendly and joyful, with a nurturing feel to it. We want others to feel and know that they are seen, understood, and heard. But the tone of voice also has the job of making people feel comfortable, both through our social media content and website alike. BetterPaw wants to tell a story that has personality, and that personality has to match with rescue dog owners. They have a different approach than regular dog owners even though they’re similar. The tone of voice is to be trusting and invite others to engage in a community. Where others say, “Rescue dogs are loving dogs”, we say “Sometimes your better half is on the other side of the world, and you only just realize it now.”.

Testing Report

  • Testing Goals: The objective of this testing protocol is to thoroughly evaluate the functionality, usability, and effectiveness of the website aimed at providing a community platform for rescue dog owners and generating personalized training plans for rescue dogs. The primary focus is to ensure that the website effectively communicates its purpose and meets the needs of the target audience. We want to find out what we could improve in terms of UI/UX, what works well in our website currently and whether we communicate our brand idenity through our website.
  • Testing Participants: For our testing we have chosen six participants, all women who own rescue dogs/are interested in adopting rescue dogs and have an interest in training plans for dogs.
  • Testing Setting: The testing of our brand was done both on laptop and mobile, respectively four tests on desktop format and two on mobile format. The tests took place on the campus/ at home, and we made sure to have a stable internet connection, comfortable seating arrangements, and quiet surroundings to minimize distractions.
  • Testing Protocol: Each participant will be briefed on the purpose of the testing and provided with necessary instructions, participants will be asked to perform the assigned tasks while verbalizing their thoughts and actions, observers will record participants' interactions, noting any difficulties, confusion, or positive experiences. The main tasks ivolved: Registration and Profile Setup on the website, Exploring Community Features, Creating a Personalized Training Plan, Interacting with the Community, Providing Feedback on the overall feel of the website, Finding Resources and Support, Finding Testimonials and Finding our Contact Information.
  • Testing results - Positive and negative aspects of the UI/UX that have been identified, ranked by importance.
  • Positive Aspects of UI/UX:
  • Clear Sign-up/Log-in Process: Testers found the sign-up/log-in process to be straightforward and obvious, indicating good usability.
  • Community Engagement: Users appreciated the community feature, finding it easy to access and valuable for seeking support and information from other users.
  • Intuitive Navigation: Users found the layout and navigation of the website to be intuitive, making it easy to find relevant information and features.
  • Training Plan Access: Testers found it easy to navigate to the personalized training plan section, including accessing the assessment test required for the plan.
  • Overall Branding and Design: Users liked the cohesive branding throughout the website, particularly mentioning the paw prints and how it contributes to a unified brand identity.
  • Functional Buttons: Testers found the buttons to be noticeable, functional, and well-matched with the overall design and color scheme.
  • Coherent Visual Design: The website features a cohesive color scheme and aesthetics, creating a visually appealing and inviting environment.
  • Social Media and Contact Accessibility: Users appreciated the clear placement of social media links and contact information, making it easy to access.
  • Negative Aspects of UI/UX:
  • Button Functionality Issues: Users encountered issues with button functionality, particularly with the "Sign Up" button, which detracted from the overall user experience.
  • Confusing Navigation: Some users found the dynamic changes in the top bar navigation confusing, especially when the "home" button moved depending on the page, suggesting a need for more consistent navigation cues.
  • Homepage Length: A few testers mentioned that the homepage felt too long, potentially overwhelming users, although this was mitigated by the ability to access other sections easily.
  • Ambiguous Page Names: Certain page names were perceived as ambiguous or confusing, leading to difficulties in understanding their purpose and functionality.
  • Improvements that have been implemented on the final website (or that would be implemented if doing so would be too complex):
  • Consistent Navigation Bar: To address the confusion regarding the dynamic top bar, a fixed navigation bar with consistent options across all pages should be implemented, ensuring that users can easily find and navigate to different sections of the website without ambiguity.
  • Streamlined Homepage: The homepage should be redesigned to condense content and reduce clutter, ensuring a more concise and user-friendly experience while still providing access to key information. This included clearer section headings, concise summaries, and prominent call-to-action buttons to direct users to relevant sections.
  • Fixing Non-Functional Features: Ensure that all features, including the "Sign Up" page, are fully functional and operational to facilitate seamless user interactions.
  • Clarifying Page Names: Rename ambiguous page names to provide clearer labels that accurately convey the purpose and content of each page, reducing user confusion.
  • Navigation Bar Drop-Down Button for Mobile: Adjust the Drop-Down button for the navigation bar on mobile so users can always navigate the pages of the website and do not have to scroll all the way up to the beginning of the page, for a better user experience.

Validation of Assumptions

We decided that the website would be focused on the following aspects: giving rescue dog owners the space to train their dogs when it is more convenient for them and fostering a helpful and supportive community. This was decided based on all the interviews that were done with the target audience. These dog owners had rescue dogs who could not get the training that they needed from the standard puppy school in their vicinity. The fixed training that was given at these schools was not something they could fit in their busy schedules, or their lessons could not be applied to their rescue dogs as opposed to other dogs, due to their difficulties such as anxiety and trauma. The product that BetterPaw offers its customers is the Behavioral Assessment Test where the customers get a personalized training plan for their pet for €40 (which is the estimated average price the target audience agreed on). The service hereby is the opportunity to talk to a coach and expert in the weekly check-in sessions that the customers can plan whenever they want. Additionally, customers can share their advice and tips with each other and the website which is the community aspect. This choice was ultimately made because the target audience expressed their desire to have (tailored) training for their rescue dogs and have more people who also had the same issues as they did around them. Moreover, this online schedule offers them freedom that is not available in traditional schools.
